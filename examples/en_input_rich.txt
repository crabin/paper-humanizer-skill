In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, cybersecurity threats are becoming increasingly sophisticated. It is worth noting that traditional intrusion detection systems often struggle to keep pace with the ever-changing nature of network attacks. It can be observed that existing methods face significant challenges when dealing with imbalanced datasets, which represents a critical gap in the current literature.

To address this issue, this paper aims to propose an innovative framework based on an improved WGAN-GP architecture. The proposed method leverages the powerful capabilities of generative adversarial networks and seamlessly integrates them with advanced deep learning techniques. Furthermore, the method holistically addresses the multifaceted challenges of intrusion detection in a comprehensive and robust manner. Additionally, an adaptive threshold mechanism is designed to further enhance the model's performance, thereby achieving optimal detection results.

The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly improves upon existing baselines across multiple evaluation metrics. It is worth noting that on the NSL-KDD dataset, our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance, clearly validating the effectiveness and superiority of the proposed framework. Moreover, the results show that the method exhibits robust performance under various challenging conditions, including scenarios with highly imbalanced class distributions.

In conclusion, the proposed method represents a significant advancement in the field of network intrusion detection. It not only addresses the critical challenges of class imbalance but also paves the way for more effective and efficient detection systems. Based on the above analysis, it can be concluded that this work has far-reaching implications for cybersecurity research and practice. Future work will explore extending the proposed framework to other domains and further optimizing the model architecture to achieve even better performance.
